Thoughts?

Honestly, the Japanese do know how to really flesh out their works. I have notably seen some wonderful animation, character design, and character development in the Senki Zesshou Symphogear series. That's really the only anime I've ever invested my time into well enough. I've finished Season 1, G, and GX as of this writing, and am going through AXZ whenever I feel like watching an episode or 2.

As a whole, anime is unique compared to whatever we get in the west. I don't have much to say about western animation other than how great something like Smiling Friends is compared to other modern animated comedy. Plus, Nickelodeon is still using a 23-year-old sponge "to wash dishes" that's way past its use cycle, and has been for 18 years. Don't consider me a film/television critic (because I would be a horrible one), but I feel like we're always falling behind on something compared to the Japanese. That can be applied for many things... like car designing and engineering; GM is nothing compared to Toyota with how well they design their cars.

That being said, there are a good amount of people who don't like anime, and I can really see why. Most of the stuff you see is about a horny late-teenage man trying to gather as many girls as he can just to... have sex with, presumably. This is what is called harem anime, or as I call it, hot garbage. Don't get me wrong, there are harem animes/mangas in which their characters deserve much better, like Date A Live. To be honest, I have not watched the anime, but I have skimmed through the english translations of Volume 1 and 2. Basically this guy named Shido has to seal powers of a spirit (Tohka) by locking lips with her. Honestly, the romance part could be cute to some, but is there a less suggestive way you could pull that off...? Oh well, not like I'll invest more time into this series anyway. I've heard Kurumi's got some great character design, aside from the fact that Date A Live is still a fucking harem.

It's okay if you don't like something, but please, for the love of god, don't try to bash someone for it. I cannot count how many people who've turned away from me because I said that I liked anime. Either it was an edgy high schooler who posts offensive memes with ear-piercing audio tracks, or it was someone who would've been enjoyable to talk with. This would probably fit the Experience page more, but ah well. Just... think twice and accept people for what they do or who they are... unless they're just too much of a douchebag. It's seemingly harder to find nice people these days, isn't it?

Japan in general has some weird stuff going on, too. I'm not particularly well versed in just how weird their stuff is, but that doesn't mean it isn't particularly obvious. But if you know what to watch and if you find something that'll fit you in at least some way, you could really enjoy it. I particularly liked the sci-fi and violence aspects of Symphogear, which somewhat resembles a more modern world and how combat would work... both realistically and non-realistically.

Then... why do you bother?

Because I had a lot of leisure time during Spring Break 2022 (which was the entire first week in April for me). I was cleaning up my area just a bit, then decided to get into Symphogear. Debating on whether or not I should have kept cleaning, but oh well. I knew a couple of people who've watched it, and suddenly it just came to mind.

Watching anime is something I normally don't do. However, I do look into small snippets and characters from time to time, which are some kind of a coping mechanism for me. I like the designs, I like the characters, and I like how I can relate to some of them, like Tsubasa Kazanari. Plus, I just get bored super easily.
